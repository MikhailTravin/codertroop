Let's go!
Step 1
Defining the goals of site development, Conducting research
At this stage of site creation it is very important to understand why and for whom you develop a web-resource.

Without the right goals and objectives you can not get the necessary site or can, but even in the process of creating you will realize that you will not get what you wanted.

It is also important to research your competitors and compiling a scheme: what functionality they provide to users, what design and structure have.
Step 2
Development of the technical assignment
The designation of the target customers of the site, as well as the overall mission;

Structure in the form of a scheme consisting of the main sections, subsections and an approximate number of pages;

Module wishes;

Design Description;

What technology to use (type of CMS, scripting libraries, will there be a mobile version, etc.);

The procedure for providing, processing or creating graphic and textual information;

Technical requirements for the site;
Step 3
Creation of site design layout
Providing an agreed number of different concepts for the main page layout;

Finalization and coordination of the selected version of the concept;

Creating layouts of internal pages (categories, goods / services, contacts, etc.);

Finalizing and approving each of the typical pages;

Visual content (banners, photos, illustrations);
Step 4
Printed by
At this stage there is purely technical work by our layout designer and programmer:

"Understandable" code - the smaller and more competent the code is, the faster the site will load. Validity (correctness) - allows a third-party specialist to easily deal with the code. Cross-browser layout (Cross-browser) - adequate display of the site in all kinds of browsers (IE, Chrome, Firefox, Opera, etc.).
Step 5
Implementation
CMS - a site management system, which includes an administrative panel, which, in turn, has the functions of managing the content on the site. Content through the admin panel is loaded very simply, like a Word document. Stretch includes configuring the layout under the parameters of the engine, the CSS itself and the path to the modules of the engine in HTML.

As a result of these several stages of work we will get a fully working site, but without any content.
Step 6
Site content
Without text and photos, the site has a rather conventional value. The customer provides the information and fills the site with text and pictures or we do it (depending on the agreement). If the customer does it himself, he is provided with instructions for working with CMS.

All materials are best given to write a professional copywriter, as this text will read the customers.
